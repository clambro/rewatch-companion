# AI Writing Tells

Use these references as background when diagnosing AI-coded prose. They are not rules to apply mechanically.

## Common Patterns

- Formulaic structure: AI-generated prose often repeats predictable templates, including clean introductions, neat body paragraphs, and tidy conclusions.
- Repetitive paragraph rhythm: Generated drafts can fall into repeated setup / explanation / takeaway patterns.
- Smooth but generic voice: The prose may be correct and polished while lacking uneven emphasis, judgment, or specificity.
- Over-formal register: AI text often leans into dense, academic, informational phrasing and recurring bundles.
- Recycled transitions and stock phrases: Common offenders include “it is important to note,” “moreover,” “furthermore,” “not merely,” “not just,” and “in conclusion.”
- Low variation: Similar sentence lengths, similar paragraph sizes, and repeated syntactic structures can make the writing feel machine-smoothed.

## How To Use This

Do not accuse text of being AI-written. Treat these patterns as editing signals:

- If many paragraphs have the same shape, vary the argumentative work each paragraph performs.
- If a sentence explains why the previous sentence matters, see whether the previous sentence can be made strong enough to stand.
- If a conclusion only summarizes, cut or replace it with a final turn.
- If a paragraph sounds broadly applicable to any essay, make it specific or remove it.
