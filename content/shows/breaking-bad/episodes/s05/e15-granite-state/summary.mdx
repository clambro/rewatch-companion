“Granite State” strips Walt’s supposed escape down to sterile logistics and humiliating dependence, turning disappearance into a holding pattern rather than release. Ed’s vacuum operation reduces flight to procedure, exposing how little of Walt’s old command survives without an audience, while Saul’s decision to become “a civilian” marks the end of the fantasy that identities can be endlessly revised without cost. In New Hampshire, cash, chemotherapy, and even the wedding ring become inert remnants of the roles they once served, proving that money cannot become care and shelter cannot become life. Meanwhile the world continues without Walt in harsher, more procedural forms: Todd and Lydia industrialize the blue meth business through Jesse’s captivity, Skyler is trapped inside legal exposure and coerced narrative, and Walt Jr.’s refusal to accept money destroys the last provider script Walt can still imagine. The episode’s key reversal comes when Gretchen and Elliott publicly reduce Walt to a name he merely contributed, which reactivates his oldest grievance over Gray Matter and gives Heisenberg one final, bitter story to inhabit.
