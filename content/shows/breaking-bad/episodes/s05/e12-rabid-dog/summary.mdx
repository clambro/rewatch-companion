“Rabid Dog” is an episode about delayed combustion and failed management, where Jesse survives the gasoline attack only to become the object everyone else tries to classify and control. The title’s “rabid dog” metaphor captures the episode’s central cruelty: Jesse is treated as evidence, leverage, liability, or prey by Walt, Hank, Saul, Marie, and Skyler, each of whom wants a clean, instrumental solution that avoids confronting what Walt has made of him. The episode’s form resists immediate payoff, opening on Walt’s dark search through a violated house and turning the missing fire into a problem of explanation rather than spectacle. Walt’s lies about the gasoline, Hank’s secret custody and wired sting, Marie’s poison fantasy, and Skyler’s exhausted push to remove the threat all show how domestic spaces have become sites of damage control, where care and revenge are indistinguishable from procedure. Jesse’s terror at Civic Plaza is not irrational but learned, because Walt has trained him to read hidden violence inside ordinary appearances; Hank’s surveillance fails because it requires Jesse to behave like stable evidence rather than traumatized prey. When Jesse abandons the sting and threatens Walt from a payphone, the episode strips away the last possibility of private repair and pushes Walt toward outsourced violence, making Todd and Jack’s crew the final expression of his desire for a clean disposal he can still pretend he did not choose.
