“Buyout” treats the aftermath of Drew Sharp’s death as the moment when the show’s criminal logic turns from spectacular action to residue, disposal, and moral adaptation. The episode’s quiet procedural labor, Todd’s chillingly businesslike rationalization, and Jesse’s physical recoil show how murder is absorbed into operations, with the “sanctity of the operation” becoming the protected value. Against Jesse and Mike’s attempt to convert stolen methylamine into an exit, Walt hears not enough money but too little empire, revealing that money has stopped functioning for him as a finish line and become a measure of authorship, scale, and grievance. His Gray Matter resentment reframes the buyout as another theft of recognition, while the domestic dinner scene and his claim that the business is all he has left expose how fully criminal ambition has replaced the provider story. By the time Mike ties him up at Vamonos Pest, the episode has made containment and settlement look like obsolete languages for a man who now wants continuity, control, and empire rather than closure.
