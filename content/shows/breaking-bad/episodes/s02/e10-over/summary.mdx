“Over” argues that remission strips away Walt’s medical alibi and leaves only appetite: when the crisis recedes, he converts relief into coercion, turning a celebration into a domination test with Walt Jr., treating Hank’s boundary-setting as a challenge to his authority, and letting the backyard overflow literalize his inability to contain himself. The house repairs extend the same impulse inward, as Walt attacks dry rot with a fantasy of clean excision, seeking a domestic and bodily cure that can isolate damage, restore control, and make his need for action feel like purpose. Against Walt’s brittle territoriality, Jesse and Jane’s scenes offer a fragile counterpoint of intimacy under interruption, where private recognition is exposed to surveillance, embarrassment, and conditional secrecy, and Jane’s small “apology” gesture becomes a modest, makeshift repair rather than a true resolution. The episode’s final hardware-store beat completes the pattern by showing Walt recognizing amateur meth cooks not as a market problem but as unauthorized copies of himself, turning technical expertise into a first crude claim to authorship, territory, and dominion, and revealing that his real issue is not survival but the need to make the world bear his mark.
