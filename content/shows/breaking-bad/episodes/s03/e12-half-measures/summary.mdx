“Half Measures” argues that the episode’s problem is not indecision but the fantasy that a temporary fix, warning, or negotiated pause can stand in for a real solution. Saul’s jail scheme, Mike’s desert warning, Gus’s “no more children” settlement, and even Hank and Marie’s hospital bargain all create the shape of closure while leaving the underlying violence intact. Mike’s confession about his own “half measure” is the episode’s moral hinge, but Walt mishears it as a challenge to be more decisive rather than a warning against mistaking performance for justice. His car attack and execution of the surviving dealer do save Jesse in the moment, yet the rescue is also the culmination of Walt’s growing appetite for full intervention, where decisive violence becomes a way to feel morally clear. The episode’s central irony is that Walt does the right thing for the wrong reasons, and in doing so discovers the dangerous power of calling retaliation help.
