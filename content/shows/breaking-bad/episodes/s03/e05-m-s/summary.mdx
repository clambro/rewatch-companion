“Más” frames Gus’s superlab as a seductive enlargement of criminal life, turning scale, cleanliness, and infrastructure into a form of recruitment that flatters Walt’s technical pride while quietly reclassifying him as an employee inside Gus’s disciplined apparatus. The episode’s core tension is between this managerial fantasy of a cleaner empire and the mess it cannot erase: Walt’s dependence on self-justifying “providing,” Jesse’s immediate recognition that he is being cut out and demoted, and Hank’s backward-moving investigation, which shows that ordinary material traces like an RV, a mother, and a photograph still puncture elaborate concealment. Saul’s office translates everything into value and exposure, exposing how quickly loyalty becomes fungible once Gus’s larger machine appears, while Jesse’s windshield smash restores visible damage to a world trying to pretend it has been streamlined. The episode’s spatial logic, with public rooms hiding private systems and hidden rooms demanding disciplined labor, makes “más” mean not just more money or more territory, but a transfer of authority that enlarges the operation by reducing people to functions.
