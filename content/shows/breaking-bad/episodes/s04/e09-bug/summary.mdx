“Bug” argues that in _Breaking Bad_, observation is never neutral and rarely mastery: every attempt to read, track, audit, or manage another person becomes a form of exposure. Hank’s GPS work, Skyler’s strategic incompetence at the IRS, Gus’s dinner-table test of Jesse, and Walt’s tracker on Jesse all turn surfaces into evidence, but the evidence keeps pointing back at the watcher’s own need for control. The episode’s key tension is between legibility and power, as characters use routines, paperwork, hospitality, and surveillance to convert hidden systems into usable knowledge, only to discover that seeing more does not stabilize their authority. Jesse’s refusal to become readable on Walt’s terms, and his final physical rejection of the tracker, makes the episode’s central reversal explicit: the object meant to produce trust and obedience becomes proof that Walt’s “partnership” is coercion.
