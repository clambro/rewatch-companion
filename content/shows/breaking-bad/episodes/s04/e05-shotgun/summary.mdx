“Shotgun” is an episode about reassignment: Jesse is placed in the passenger seat and then given, through Gus’s manufactured shotgun ambush, a role that feels chosen even though it is carefully staged, while Walt spends the hour trying to force his way back into arrangements already functioning without him. The episode treats usefulness as something produced by management, not self-assertion, so Jesse’s competence becomes legible only when Gus engineers the occasion and Mike grudgingly acknowledges his limited status, whereas Walt’s need for credit turns every attempt at concealment into exposure. His panic over Jesse’s disappearance, his failed struggle with the methylamine barrel, and his insistence on correcting Hank at dinner all reveal the same hunger to be indispensable and recognized, a hunger Skyler has learned to convert into practical cover through the car wash and domestic arrangement. The key reversal is that Jesse gains a stable, if manipulated, function under Gus, while Walt’s vanity reactivates Hank’s investigation, turning the Los Pollos Hermanos napkin from a trivial clue into evidence and showing how his refusal to let secrecy remain anonymous keeps feeding the danger around him.
