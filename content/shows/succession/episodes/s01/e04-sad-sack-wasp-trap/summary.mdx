“Sad Sack Wasp Trap” argues that _Succession_ treats public ceremony as a machine of reclassification: Kendall’s attempt to turn Logan’s stroke into a controlled transition collapses because his office is always borrowed territory, vulnerable to Logan’s bodily presence and symbolic force, while the foundation gala transforms frailty into authority and ambition into exposure. Logan’s wheelchair does not diminish him so much as sharpen the room’s attention, letting weakness become a stage for command when he reclaims Waystar and publicly admits Tom into the family, proving that standing, applause, and naming are corporate procedures disguised as sentiment. Against that polished surface, the cruises storyline reveals the buried rot beneath Waystar’s philanthropy, showing how misconduct, payoffs, and scapegoating are the institution’s hidden infrastructure. The episode’s key vocabulary is contamination, witness, and placement: Kendall needs external certification, Tom receives a poisoned inheritance, Greg survives by passing danger along, and Gerri sees that the real question is who will hold the blame.
