“I Went to Market” treats Thanksgiving as a market in which loyalty, humiliation, access, fear, and evidence are traded but never stably priced, collapsing the supposed separation between family ritual and corporate power. Kendall’s vote of no confidence depends on converting private disgust and fear of Logan into public allegiance, but the episode shows how hard it is to make hesitation harden into support when Logan’s atmospheric authority still structures every room. Tom’s prenup anxiety and Shiv’s cool evasions frame marriage as another contract fantasy that cannot protect him from dependency or humiliation, while Greg’s shredding and secret copying turn paperwork into the episode’s real currency, linking abasement to leverage. Ewan functions as a hard anti-Logan who nonetheless refuses to become Kendall’s clean ally, underscoring that opposition to Logan is not liberation from Roy logic. The dinner sequence turns the family meal into an audition of status, where watching Logan watch them matters as much as anything said, and Logan’s cranberry-sauce attack on Iverson becomes the decisive public demonstration that his weakness has not displaced his power, only made his cruelty into usable evidence.
