“Which Side Are You On?” argues that Kendall’s coup fails not because the board math is wrong, but because he cannot turn arithmetic into durable public allegiance in Logan’s presence. The episode treats the no-confidence vote as a test of whether private disgust can survive being seen, and it shows each sibling failing in a distinct way: Kendall overestimates procedure and underestimates the need to physically inhabit the room, Roman can diagnose bullshit but cannot supply a real pitch or a stable position, and Shiv keeps a political exit route open because she still believes mobility can substitute for commitment. The title’s labor-song question becomes a corporate demand for visible, punishable side-taking, and the episode uses that pressure to expose how Roy power works as intimidation, humiliation, and exposure rather than persuasion. Ewan’s refusal is the sharpest complication because his contempt for Logan is real but not convertible into support for Kendall, making the boardroom tie feel like the family’s larger pattern in miniature: opposition to Logan repeatedly collapses when it has to become an act instead of a posture.
