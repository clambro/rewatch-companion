## The episode's move

The pilot is not mainly asking who will inherit Waystar. It is asking what the Roy children have already inherited. Logan's birthday should be a private ritual, but the episode turns it into a corporate weather system: every gesture, joke, gift, and insult becomes a test of proximity to power.

## Kendall's false coronation

Kendall begins the series acting as if the succession has already happened. The Vaulter negotiation exposes the gap between his idea of himself and the authority others are willing to grant him. He can speak the language of the future, but he cannot make anyone believe Logan has truly left the room.

## Why it matters

Viewed after the finale, "Celebration" looks less like a starting line than a diagnosis. The children do not spend the series trying to escape Logan's system. They keep trying to prove they can operate it without being destroyed by it.
