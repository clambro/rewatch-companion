“Secession” argues that Kendall’s public break from Logan changes the battlefield without escaping Logan’s grammar of power: succession becomes secession only in the sense that each side builds an improvised court using the same habits of command, control, and contamination. Kendall’s Rava apartment headquarters is a parody of revolutionary space, a “sealed unit” fantasy that exposes his dependence on messy, inherited materials, while his managerial language collapses into performative corporate noise that still makes others move around him. Greg is valuable not as moral support but as logistical witness, translating Kendall’s rupture into usable action, even as the court’s poverty shows in the way Naomi, Rava, and Lisa register the revolt as invasion, appropriation, or belated access. By contrast, Logan’s airborne court proves that authority does not require stable architecture, only hierarchy, relay, and diagnosis: SUVs and planes become command centers because Logan remains the body around which others orient. Roman disqualifies himself through hedged ambition, Shiv fails because her independence is still routed through paternal assignment and bad timing, and Tom survives by staying useful to whatever force can still confer leverage. Gerri’s interim CEO appointment is the episode’s key irony, a competent procedural patch that names a leader without creating belief, confirming that the company can install a title but not replace the old center of gravity.
