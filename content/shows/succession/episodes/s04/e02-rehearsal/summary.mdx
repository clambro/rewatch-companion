“Rehearsal” argues that the Roy siblings’ attempt at collective independence is not defeated in public so much as compromised by private attachments that Logan still knows how to exploit. Their GoJo alliance is a temporary convergence of separate injuries, and Kendall’s concealed call with Matsson, Shiv’s rage over Tom, and Roman’s birthday texting all reveal how little shared interior the bloc really has. The karaoke-room confrontation crystallizes the episode’s central claim: Logan’s line, “I love you, but you are not serious people,” is at once manipulative and diagnostically true, because the children keep treating opposition to him as proof of selfhood. Connor, through his grotesque but lucid self-knowledge, becomes the episode’s clearest observer of this emotional economy, while the Kerry subplot offers an administrative version of the same downward delegation of humiliation through Tom and Greg. The ending matters because Logan does not need to win the siblings as a group if he can isolate Roman, whose need for paternal selection makes him the child still available to be pulled back in through a job-shaped form of intimacy, especially via Matsson and ATN.
