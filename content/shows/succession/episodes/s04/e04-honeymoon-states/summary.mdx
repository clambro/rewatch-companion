“Honeymoon States” is a chamber-piece about how Logan’s death does not end his rule so much as convert it into procedure: his apartment becomes a boardroom, a mourning space, and a family sorting room where every doorway marks a temporary claim to power. The episode’s central device is the scrap of paper from Logan’s safe, a legally useless but emotionally decisive document that functions less as proof of succession than as a Rorschach test for the survivors, turning grief back into the language of inheritance, rank, and exclusion. The siblings’ brief solidarity over Logan’s obituary and private memory is real, but it collapses the moment the paper appears, because their bond only holds while no one has to accept a lower position. Shiv is especially stranded, cut out of the formal co-CEO arrangement and isolated further by the private fact of her progressing pregnancy, while Tom, Greg, Connor, and Marcia all reveal their own versions of opportunistic scrambling, transactional shelter-seeking, and administrative power. The episode’s decisive irony is Kendall’s closing move: after publicly accepting the appearance of shared leadership with Roman, he privately blackmails Hugo into attacking Logan’s reputation, showing that the new regime begins not with stability but with secrecy, coercion, and a betrayal of its own clean optics.
